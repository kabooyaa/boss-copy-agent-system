---

**POST BODY (copy this exactly):**

The reason your social posts get 3 likes and a tumbleweeds emoji?

You're starting with the wrong word.

Most business owners open their posts with "We" or "I" or "Our."

The algorithm reads that and goes: "Boring. Next."

The thumb doesn't stop. The reach tanks. The post dies.

I spent 6 hours in a webinar last week studying why certain posts go viral while identical ones get buried.

The answer is a 5-step framework called S.T.A.R.T.

Here's the breakdown:

S — STOP SCROLL
Your first line has one job: make the thumb stop. Use a bold claim, a shocking number, or a direct challenge. Never start with "We."

T — TALK ABOUT THE PROBLEM
Agitate the pain. Make your reader feel seen. Use "you" language. 2-4 sentences.

A — ALIGN WITH AUDIENCE
Show you understand them. "I used to think..." or "Most business owners make this mistake..." 2-3 sentences.

R — RESOLVE THE PROBLEM
Give real value. Not a teaser. Not "DM me for the secret." Actually help them. 3-5 sentences.

T — TELL THEM WHAT TO DO
Clear CTA. But here's the key: DO NOT put your link in the caption. The algorithm suppresses posts with links. Put it in the first comment instead.
